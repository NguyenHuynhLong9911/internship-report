### Lessons and experience regarding the role of AI

After the event, I understood that AI is more than a tool for generating content or answering questions. When supplied with appropriate data, context, and tools, AI can assist with information analysis, automate parts of a workflow, and coordinate complex tasks. However, output quality depends directly on context quality, system design, and evaluation criteria.

AI should support human decision-making rather than completely replace it. For use cases that affect finance, operations, or users, AI-generated results must be verified and constrained by clear guardrails. A multi-agent system is also valuable only when each agent has a defined responsibility, appropriate access permissions, and end-to-end monitoring.

The key experience I gained was to begin with the real problem and define the required data and expected outcome before choosing an AI model or tool. This principle also applies to SmartStudy: AI should remain grounded in the learner's documents, operate within a clear scope, and support learning instead of producing unverified answers.

### Key Takeaways

- AI quality depends heavily on the amount and quality of context provided to the system.
- CloudFront can serve as an important foundation for performance, reliability, security, and cost optimization.
- LLM output can still vary even when deterministic settings are used, so evaluation and mitigation strategies are important.
- Multi-agent systems can support complex enterprise workflows when designed with guardrails and compliance in mind.
- The event helped connect AI concepts with practical cloud and software engineering use cases.
